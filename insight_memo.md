# NSE Sectoral Rotation — Insight Memo
### What 3 years of NSE data tells us about where investors should have been

---

## Finding 1 — AUTO crushed everything, IT was a rollercoaster

AUTO was the standout sector of this entire period. It came in at #2 in 2022 (+22.9%), then went #1 in both 2023 (+45.27%) and 2024 (+56.75%). No other sector came close to that consistency.

IT tells the opposite story. It was the worst sector in 2022, losing 33.34%. Then flipped to +33.96% in 2023. If you panicked and sold IT in early 2022 and never came back, you missed a massive recovery.

The gap between staying in IT vs switching to AUTO in 2023 alone was over 11 percentage points. That's the whole point of rotation.

---

## Finding 2 — Some sectors are basically the same bet

BANKING and FINANCE move almost in lockstep (correlation 0.63). So does ENERGY and METALS (0.63). Holding both in a pair gives you very little actual diversification — you're just doubling down on the same theme.

The most independent pair in the data is ENERGY and FMCG (0.21). These two barely influence each other, which makes them genuinely useful to hold together.

If you want a diversified 2-sector portfolio, FMCG + AUTO or PHARMA + ENERGY makes more sense than BANKING + FINANCE.

---

## Finding 3 — HIGH returns don't always mean GOOD returns

AUTO 2023 had the best Sharpe proxy of any sector any year (0.53) — meaning it gave the highest return per unit of risk taken. PHARMA 2023 came second (0.43).

Meanwhile METALS 2024 returned +53.99% but had a Sharpe of only 0.27 — the ride was extremely volatile. You'd have made money but lost sleep.

FMCG was boring but dependable — low volatility across all 3 years. Good for investors who don't want surprises.

---

## Recommendation 1 — Rotation beat doing nothing

| Year | Top Sector | Return | NIFTY Return |
|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 2022 | BANKING | +25.66% | ~+4% |
| 2023 | AUTO | +45.27% | ~+20% |
| 2024 | AUTO | +56.75% | ~+9% |

Someone who simply moved into the prior year's best performing sector at the start of each year would have significantly outperformed a passive NIFTY investor all 3 years running. The strategy isn't perfect but the data clearly favours active rotation over sitting still.

---

## Recommendation 2 — Watch AUTO and PHARMA going into 2025

AUTO has been #1 for 2 straight years with strong risk adjusted returns both times. This isn't random — it reflects real structural tailwinds like EV adoption, strong domestic sales and export growth.

PHARMA has been in the top 4 every single year of this analysis. It's not flashy but it's reliable. US export approvals and domestic healthcare spending keep it supported.

These are the two sectors worth watching most closely heading into 2025.
